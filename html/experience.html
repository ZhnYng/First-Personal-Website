My Experience

Academic

Although I have been in Singapore Polytechnic for only 6 weeks, I have noticed many differences comparing it to the education system I was in for the past 10 years. Regardless of these changes, my values continue to serve me well as they did years ago. I am able to keep up with assignments, be participative in class, and enjoy being in the course I have chosen so far as it has what I expected to be learning.

Social

Meeting new people and making new friends, are always the best part of entering a new environment. My classmates and I frequent foodcourts near the school of computing. There are also many clubs and CCAs available in Singapore Polytechnic(SP), where we can meet more people with the same interest. Going for events outside of academics can also be very meaningful, such doing social work to benefit others, or just to unwind. Below are the CCAs that I am in.

Other courses

DCITP

The DCITP is a semester long programme designed to give students exposure to a large range of jobs and courses in the Infocomm landscape and help you decide which occupations, job roles, career pathways and employment opportunities in the various Infocomm sectors to choose from. A strong foundation in programming and IT fundamentals can be expected from the DCITP.

DISM

Being a Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM) student gives you the competitive edge for pursuing in your future studies and careers due to the relevancy of the course. You can expect an interesting curriculum that covers offensive attacks, defensive methods and investigative skills.

DIT

There continues to be a strong demand for IT professionals in Software Development, User Experience (UX) design, and in Immersive Simulation Technologies such as Augmented and Virtual Reality in Singapore. The Diploma in Information Technology (DIT) course is specially curated to focus on Software & Applications development.
